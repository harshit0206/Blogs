Identify unique location elements in warehouse

Implement an efficient warehouse location mapping after choosing a warehouse storage solution (identifying and naming all the shelving positions).

A warehouse location numbering system is just a set of numbers that identify the various locations inside a warehouse. To make it easier to locate goods, the warehouse storage numbering system should be as detailed as feasible. Each element of storage location, such as aisle, shelf or level, and position on the shelf from left to right, should be identified by the location system.

The structure and organization of a warehouse are reflected in most warehouse location IDs, which include four to six fields.


Let’s look at the most common nomenclature fields

  • Section or Zone

    Zone is used to designate a section of the warehouse that is unique from others. For example, zones might be used for bulk storage, receiving, staging, etc.

  • Aisle

    Each aisle should be identified with a number. If there are more than 9 aisles then start numbering with 01. This will give up to 99 aisles.

  • Rack or Bay

    Rack or bay is the space between the rack uprights. Aisle numbering and bay numbering is the same thing.

  • Level or shelf

    Characters are used to identify warehouse level schemes. In the vast majority of cases, the floor level will be Level A. The following levels are frequently referred to as B/C/D/E/F.

  • Position or Slot

    The number of places on a shelf or rack is usually limited to nine. The approach of numbering positions with two digits is favored (01, 02, 03...).
