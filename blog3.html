Barcodes for Healthcare Industry

In Healthcare Industry, Barcodes provide an easy and cost-effective solution to major problems such as difficulty for the industry to manage information, tracking materials, inventory complications, and patient validation.

Along with the safety of patients, the healthcare industry has more work to do like to manage information. These activities are not only time consuming but, if these things will be done manually, there is a chance for error.

In the past few decades, the technology has developed a lot, due to which we have grown better with advancements in the field of patient safety. One of the easy and cost-effective solutions to all the complex problems that run inside the healthcare industry is the use of barcode technology. Use of barcode saves time and allows industry to spent time on increasing efficiency instead of entering data manually.

Use of barcodes in the healthcare industry helps in organizing the patient admittance process, track their medication, and identification of patients.

Features of using barcodes in healthcare industry
  • Inventory control
    • In the healthcare industry, barcodes are used to save time in complex inventory control.
    • Barcodes help in managing the location of inventories, so that the right materials are available when and where you need them.
    • With barcode scanning, the system is enabled to automatically re-order products when they reach a specific inventory level.
  • Tracking Materials
    • Barcodes can track medication given to a particular patient and also identifies the clinician who used it.
    • Barcodes can be used to track a drug back to the manufacturer so that the defective drug can be refunded.
  • Barcode wristbands
    • At the time of patient's admission, barcode wristbands are created with their specific information, which is continuously updated on the basis of the patient’s need.
    • Barcode labels are of great help to doctors and nurses to track patient’s information as all the patient’s records like medical report, medication and specimen samples are tagged with barcode labels.
    • Barcodes verify that the patient being treated is the right patient and that the treatment is appropriate.
