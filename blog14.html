Pharmacode Barcode


Pharmacode is used in the pharmaceutical industry as a packing control system designed to prevent packing errors. The purpose of pharmacode is to ensure that the pharmaceutical product package is the correct product, along with the correct supporting documentation. It is a robust symbol, designed to survive printing errors and post-production damage.

Barcoding's advantages in the pharmaceutical industry include:
  • Inventory tracking and controls
  • Logistics and materials tracking
  • Tracking of reusable equipments and supplies
  • Retracing the supply chain

The FDA Barcode Rule mandates that items be labelled with a linear barcode that includes the product's National Drug Code on the other hand the DSCSA mandates that items be labelled with a DataMatrix barcode that includes the product's National Drug Code, Serial Number, Lot Number, and Expiry Date. Neither UPC-A nor DataMatrix can satisfy both sets of requirements. Also, not all pharmaceutical products require two barcodes.


Use of the UPC-A Barcode

UPC-A is a linear barcode. It holds a maximum of 12 digits, and therefore can only carry a 12-digit GTIN. It cannot carry any secondary information like expiry date or serial number. UPC-A can be used to mark pharmaceutical products to support either retail Point-of-Sale and/or the FDA Barcode Rule. UPC-A is a font from the EAN/UPC family that is often used to label products sold in retail stores.



Use of the DataMatrix Barcode

Pharmaceutical products are marked with a 2D DataMatrix barcode consisting of the National Drug Code, Serial Number, Lot Number, and Expiry Date. DataMatrix has more capacity and can contain the GTIN plus secondary information. Therefore, it can carry all four required data elements stated above.

GTIN 00321012345678
EXP 311225
SN 1001234
LOT 9876543210DCBA


Step-by-Step Instructions to generate Barcode

To mark UPC-A and DataMatrix barcode on a product, follow the steps below to generate barcodes using DRPU Barcode Label Maker software and assure that the GTIN encoded in both barcodes is same:

  1. Assign a 12-digit GTIN to identify the product.
  2. Select Linear Barcode in DRPU Barcode Label Maker software and choose UPCA font then create barcode using 12-digit GTIN.
  3. Append the 12-digit GTIN with two leading zeros to create a GTIN-12 in 14-digit format.
    GTIN-12 :- 321012345678
    GTIN-12 in 14-digit format :- 00321012345678
  4. Select 2D Barcode in DRPU Barcode Label Maker software and choose DataMatrix font then create barcode using GTIN-12 in 14-digit format. Also encode the serial number, lot number, and expiry date in the DataMatrix in addition to the GTIN.
  5. Now print the created barcodes.
