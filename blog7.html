Barcodes for Warehouse

Many warehouses are moving to automation technologies to improve efficiency and reduce costs. Out of these technologies, barcode scanning is the most popular technology. A typical warehouse manages shipping, receiving, returns, labor tracking and packing activities that often happens simultaneously.

Barcodes are used to provide a unique identifier to each item so it becomes easy to transfer them between different locations within and outside a warehouse. As a key of efficient inventory management, it becomes a convenient and reliable way to track goods by implementing barcode technology.

Moving bulk containers and products easily through several unique steps is associated to efficiency of a warehouse. While performing inventory counts and other activities by using spreadsheets and manual data entry can lead to errors and prevents staff from responding to changes quickly.

Barcodes can be used in warehouse paperwork, for employee identification to track who did what, on products and on cartons to identify and track them. Warehouse barcode labels allow tagging each warehouse location, rack and container with a label that can be easily scanned at any time.


Barcodes provide a few unique benefits that are important for warehousing industry:
  • Better inventory management
  • Faster processing speed
  • Track employee performance
  • Reduced operational costs
  • Increased productivity
  • Error elimination
  • Enhanced accuracy
  • Improved scheduling of activities

Barcode types for Warehousing Industry

In the same warehouse, a number of different barcodes are used to identify boxes and products. Sometimes same product has multiple types of barcodes on it. These barcodes need to be captured quickly, often from a distance.


Commonly found barcodes in Warehousing Industry include:
  • Code 128

    It is a high-density code commonly found on shipment labels and supports all 128 ASCII symbols in the ASCII character set.

  • UPC

    It is used to label individual products and supports 12-digit number only.

  • EAN

    It is also used to label individual products and supports 13-digit number only.

  • MSI

    It is used for matching shelves with the products in warehouse and supports numbers only.


Implementation of a barcode system in a warehouse, adds significant value to its operations and can reduce operational costs and lead to a significant Return Of Investment by automating key steps in the inventory flow process and reducing manual data entry.
