Barcodes for Postal service


Collection, processing, transmission and delivery of mail are the primary function of Post Office. The Postal Service uses tools that reads the barcode to sort and process the mail. The barcode tells where to deliver the mail.

The Postal Barcode can be a 1D or a 2D barcode. Postal barcodes are primarily used in the postal service and can cover many properties, e.g. Sender and reciever information.

For different aspects of the mail, different kinds of barcodes are used. Also the tray and sack labels have barcodes on them.

All of these barcodes have different functions in postal service and must ensure certain specifications so that they can be read correctly by the Postal Service. Barcodes are used in the Post Office since 1965 to automate the processes.


Barcodes provide a few unique benefits that are important for Postal service:
  • Faster processing speed
  • Reduced operational costs
  • Error elimination
  • Enhanced accuracy
  • Improved scheduling of activities

Barcode types for Postal service

In the post office, a number of different barcodes are used to identify mail and parcels. Sometimes same item has multiple types of barcodes on it. These barcodes need to be captured quickly, often from a distance.


Commonly found barcodes in Postal service:


1D Barcodes


Code 93
˅


The Code 93 is primarily used in postal services to encode supplementary delivery information. It is used to encode text, numbers and special characters.


Postnet
˅


The Postnet is a 1D Barcode that is used to encode ZIP codes on US mail.


USS-93
˅


The USS-93 is a 1D Barcode that is primarily used in postal services. It can encode all 128 ASCII characters.



2D Barcode


DataMatrix
˅


The DataMatrix is a 2D barcode that can be used for encode text and numbers. This barcode can store up to 2,335 alphanumeric characters. DataMatrix codes have a good error correction and are more secure.
