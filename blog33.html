A barcode is a pattern of fairly parallel lines of variable lengths that is prominently printed on a product. Businesses and organizations may use barcode systems to manage goods, pricing, and stock levels, resulting in significant productivity and efficiency gains. The use of a barcode essentially lowers the time it takes to acquire such data in half and significantly reduces the possibility of human data input errors.

Thermal barcode label printers literally are the printing method of choice for producing barcode labels in Mac because they can print dense, sharp-edged bar codes at a particularly low cost, one label at a time or thousands at a time.

How to connect a printer with Mac
  • Make sure macOS has the most recent printer software before you start setting up your printer.
  • You don't need to check for software updates if you have a multifunction AirPrint printer.
  • To unpack the printer and connect it to your Mac, follow the instructions that came with it.
  • Use a multiport adaptor if your Mac only has one USB-C port and you wish to connect a USB printer. Connect the printer, then plug in a USB-C cable to a power supply to extend your Mac's battery life.
  • From the Apple menu, select System Preferences > Add Printer.
  • Then click Add after selecting your printer.

Thermal Barcode Printer for Mac

Thermal printing, which is ideally suited for label printing in Mac, is available in two variants. For your Mac, you may use either a thermal transfer printer or a direct thermal printer.


Direct Thermal

Heat from a thermal printhead is used to generate an image directly on the label media, which is heat-sensitive chemically treated paper. When the components of the print head come into contact with direct thermal paper, it turns black. This is the sort of printing technique you'll see on ATM, gas station, and other receipts.

Direct thermal printing employs specially coated print material that is printed directly from the printhead without the usage of a print ribbon. However, label life is limited to around six months, and direct contact with the label medium causes the printhead to wear out more quickly.


Direct thermal printers are a cost-effective option for creating barcode labels in Mac. You save money on materials because it doesn't utilize ink, toner, or a printing ribbon. In general, labels should persist as long as they aren't exposed to direct sunlight or extreme heat. In these temperatures, the paper is designed to become black, as you've probably seen if you've ever left a direct thermal receipt in your car on a hot day.

If your barcode labels will be used primarily inside, a direct thermal printer is a good option to consider.


Thermal Transfer

Another heat-based barcode printing process is thermal transfer. The printhead's hot element makes contact with a specific ribbon, which subsequently transmits the image to the label media, rather than immediately imprinting on the paper. The ribbons are made of a variety of materials, including wax, resin, and a mixture of both, and can perform a wide range of tasks. The wax or resin melts into the label and hardens to a tough, long-lasting finish.

Thermal transfer barcode labels can be printed on a variety of materials, including paper, film, polyester, and other synthetics, and with a variety of adhesives. Thermal transfer employs a ribbon, which adds to the cost of consumables, but it also wears down the printhead more slowly and, most importantly, produces long-lasting labels.


Thermal transfer produces long-lasting labels that resist scratches and smudging in harsh environments. It's a more expensive technology among printers for creating barcode labels, but if you need to improve the quality of your labels, the investment will save you the effort of changing illegible labels or locating and annotating everything.

There are more options in terms of features and functionality among various thermal printer kinds for Mac. You may choose from a wide selection of printers for creating barcodes, including desktop, mid-range, industrial and portable thermal barcode printers.
